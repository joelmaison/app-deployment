Title: Basketball Africa League Gender Equality Initiatives
Source: NBA Communications
URL: https://pr.nba.com/basketball-africa-league-gender-equality-initiatives/

INTRODUCTION
On March 8, 2021, in commemoration of International Women’s Day, the NBA and Basketball Africa League (BAL) announced a series of initiatives to advance gender equality and economic inclusion across Africa. These efforts build on the NBA’s global social justice programs and aim to create a more inclusive sports ecosystem.

OBJECTIVES
- Promote gender equality and inclusion in basketball and related industries.
- Address gender-based violence and support girls’ education.
- Increase female participation in basketball operations and leadership roles.

KEY ACTIONS
- Anti-Violence Campaigns: Educational workshops and participation in campaigns to combat gender-based violence.
- STEM Curriculum: Development of a Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM) sports curriculum for girls aged 14 and under to inspire future careers.
- Operational Roles: Expand opportunities for women in BAL gameday operations, including referees, scorer’s table officials, match commissioners, and announcers.
- Partnerships: Collaborate with African-owned enterprises and community organizations to minimize gender gaps and foster economic inclusion.

SIGNIFICANCE
These initiatives aim to dismantle stereotypes and barriers that prevent women from realizing their potential, ensuring that young girls and women across Africa have access to resources and opportunities both on and off the court.
