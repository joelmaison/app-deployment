Title: FIBA Africa Sub-Zones
Source: FIBA Official Website
URL: https://about.fiba.basketball/en/regions/africa/structure/sub-zones

INTRODUCTION
FIBA Africa organizes its 54 National Federations into seven sub-zones to streamline governance, competitions, and development programs across the continent.

SUB-ZONES & COUNTRIES
- ZONE 1: Algeria, Libya, Morocco, Tunisia
- ZONE 2: Cape Verde, Gambia, Guinea, Guinea-Bissau, Mali, Mauritania, Senegal, Sierra Leone
- ZONE 3: Benin, Burkina Faso, Côte d’Ivoire, Ghana, Liberia, Niger, Nigeria, Togo
- ZONE 4: Cameroon, Central African Republic, Chad, Congo, DR Congo, Equatorial Guinea, Gabon, São Tomé and Príncipe
- ZONE 5: Burundi, Egypt, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Kenya, Rwanda, Somalia, South Sudan, Sudan, Tanzania, Uganda
- ZONE 6: Angola, Botswana, Eswatini, Lesotho, Malawi, Mozambique, Namibia, South Africa, Zambia, Zimbabwe
- ZONE 7: Comoros, Djibouti, Madagascar, Mauritius, Seychelles

PURPOSE
These sub-zones facilitate regional competitions, talent development, and qualification pathways for continental and global tournaments.
