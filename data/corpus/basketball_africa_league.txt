INTRODUCTION
The Basketball Africa League (BAL) is the premier men's club basketball league in Africa, co-organized by NBA Africa and FIBA. It launched in 2019 and held its inaugural season in 2021, featuring 12 teams each season.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT
The NBA and FIBA announced the BAL on February 16, 2019, during NBA All-Star weekend. The first season—delayed by COVID-19—was played in a bubble in Kigali in May 2021, with Zamalek winning the inaugural title over US Monastir.

FORMAT & QUALIFICATION
The BAL currently features 12 teams: seven qualify directly as national champions, while five earn spots via the Road to BAL qualifiers. The regular season is split into three conferences, followed by playoffs; BAL champions qualify for the FIBA Intercontinental Cup.

KEY FACTS
- Organizing bodies: NBA Africa & FIBA
- Number of teams: 12; season typically runs March–June
- Commissioner: Amadou Gallo Fall
- Current champions (2025): Al Ahli Tripoli (1st title)
- Champions by season: 2021 Zamalek; 2022 US Monastir; 2023 Al Ahly; 2024 Petro de Luanda; 2025 Al Ahli Tripoli

NOTABLE DEVELOPMENTS
BAL expanded its group phase to three conferences (including the Kalahari Conference) starting in 2024, and continues to stage playoffs/finals in Kigali under a multi-year agreement.

MEDIA/TV PARTNERS
Games are broadcast across Africa and globally through ESPN, Canal+, and local networks, with streaming available on NBA and BAL platforms.

SOCIAL IMPACT
BAL promotes youth development, gender equality, and community engagement through grassroots programs and partnerships with NGOs.

NOTABLE TEAMS/PLAYERS
Teams: Zamalek, US Monastir, Petro de Luanda, Al Ahly, Al Ahli Tripoli
Players: Walter Hodge, Souleyman Diabate, Makrem Ben Romdhane

LOOKING AHEAD
Season 6 (2026) is planned with playoffs and finals in BK Arena, Kigali, per the long-term agreement; winners will earn a berth in the 2026 FIBA Intercontinental Cup.
