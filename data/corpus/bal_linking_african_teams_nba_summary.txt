Title: Basketball Africa League: Linking African Teams to the NBA
Source: Africa Renewal
URL: https://africarenewal.un.org/en/magazine/basketball-africa-league-linking-african-teams-nba

INTRODUCTION
The Basketball Africa League (BAL), launched in 2019 as a partnership between NBA and FIBA, is revolutionizing professional basketball in Africa. It provides a platform for African players to showcase their talent globally and fosters social impact initiatives across the continent.

LEAGUE BACKGROUND
- Founded: February 2019 in Dakar, Senegal
- First Season: May 2021 in Kigali, Rwanda
- Features: 12 club teams from across Africa
- Champions: Zamalek (Egypt) – inaugural season; US Monastir (Tunisia) – second season

PLAYER & COACH OPPORTUNITIES
- NBA Summer League: BAL players like Anas Mahmoud (Egypt) and Evans Ganapamo (CAR) participated in NBA Summer League.
- Coaching Exchange: BAL coaches invited as assistants in NBA Summer League (e.g., Francois Enyegue, Emmanuel Mavomo).
- Referee Development: BAL referees officiated NBA Summer League games.

GENDER EQUALITY INITIATIVES (BAL4HER)
- Programs for women coaches and decision-makers.
- Training opportunities for young women in basketball academies.
- BAL4HER Day: Panels, workshops, and networking to promote women in sports.

CLIMATE ACTION PROGRAMS (BAL Green)
- Threes-for-Trees Initiative: Planting three trees for every 3-pointer scored in BAL games and activations.
- Partnership with Great Green Wall: Restore 100M hectares of land, capture 250M tons of carbon, and create 10M green jobs by 2030.
- Social Media Engagement: One tree planted for every fan interaction online.

FUTURE OUTLOOK
- Season 3: Sahara Conference in Dakar, Nile Conference in Cairo, Playoffs and Finals in Kigali.
- Plans to launch women’s league and expand social impact programs.

SIGNIFICANCE
BAL is bridging African basketball with global opportunities, promoting gender equality, and driving climate action, making sports a catalyst for sustainable development.
