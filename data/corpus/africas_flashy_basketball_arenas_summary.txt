Title: Getting to Know Africa's Flashy Basketball Arenas
Source: FIBA
URL: https://www.fiba.basketball/en/news/getting-to-know-africas-flashy-basketball-arenas

INTRODUCTION
Africa has witnessed a surge in modern basketball infrastructure, with state-of-the-art arenas symbolizing a new era for the sport. These venues host major continental and global events, enhancing basketball’s profile across the continent.

KEY ARENAS & DETAILS
- Kigali Arena (Rwanda): 10,000 seats; multipurpose venue near Amahoro Stadium; facilities include fitness center, media hall, doping control room; hosted 2019 Rwandan League Playoffs and scheduled for BAL Final Round and FIBA AfroBasket.

- Cairo Stadium Indoor Halls Complex (Egypt): 16,000-seat main hall; hosted FIBA U19 Basketball World Cup (2017); built in 1991; venue for numerous continental and global events.

- Dakar Arena (Senegal): Inaugurated in 2018; hosted 2019 FIBA Women’s AfroBasket; record attendance of 15,000 fans; one of Africa’s largest basketball venues.

- Arena Kilamba (Angola): Built in 2013; capacity 13,000; hosted FIBA World Cup African Qualifiers; equipped with modern technology including electronic scoreboards and hardwood flooring.

- Salle Omnisports de Rades (Tunisia): 17,000 seats; hosted FIBA AfroBasket editions (2015, 2017) and African Men’s Handball Championship; among Africa’s largest indoor arenas.

- South Africa Venues: Ellis Park Arena (5,000 seats), Sun Arena (Pretoria), TicketPro Dome (Johannesburg); hosted NBA Africa Games (2015–2018).

SIGNIFICANCE
These arenas reflect Africa’s commitment to developing basketball infrastructure, enabling the continent to host world-class events and nurture local talent.
