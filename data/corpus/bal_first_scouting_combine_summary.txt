Title: Basketball Africa League to Hold Its First-Ever Scouting Combine
Source: Andscape
URL: https://andscape.com/features/basketball-africa-league-to-hold-its-first-ever-scouting-combine/

INTRODUCTION
The Basketball Africa League (BAL) announced its first scouting combine to identify talent for its inaugural season. The event marks a significant milestone in BAL’s mission to elevate African basketball to global standards.

EVENT DETAILS
- Dates: December 4–5, 2019
- Location: Brooklyn Nets’ practice facility, Hospital for Special Surgery Training Center, New York
- Purpose: Evaluate players through anthropometric and athletic testing, positional skill development, and 5-on-5 games in front of scouts, coaches, and African basketball executives.

KEY PARTICIPANTS
- Leadership: BAL President Amadou Gallo Fall; FIBA Africa President Anibal Manave
- Camp Director: Former NBA head coach P.J. Carlesimo
- Coaching Staff: Paul Westhead, Charlie Bell, Jaren Jackson Sr., Theo Ratliff, Will Voigt, Tom Parrotta
- Attendees: Pops Mensah-Bonsu (G League GM), Dikembe Mutombo (Hall of Famer), Luol Deng (NBA All-Star)
- Prospects: Former NBA players Jordan Adams, Rodney Carney, Yakhouba Diawara, Tim Quarterman, Chris Smith; 20 former G League players

ROSTER RULES
- Each BAL team: 12 active players + up to 4 inactive players
- Nationality requirements: 8 players from team’s home country, 4 from other African nations, 2 from outside Africa

INAUGURAL SEASON INFORMATION
- Launch: March 2020
- Host Cities: Cairo (Egypt), Dakar (Senegal), Lagos (Nigeria), Luanda (Angola), Rabat (Morocco), Monastir (Tunisia)
- Finals: Kigali, Rwanda
- Sponsors: Nike and Jordan Brand as official outfitters
- Supporters: Former U.S. President Barack Obama among notable backers

SIGNIFICANCE
The combine underscores BAL’s commitment to professionalizing basketball in Africa and creating pathways for African talent to reach global platforms.
