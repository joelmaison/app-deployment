Title: A Whole New Ball Game – Women’s Basketball in Africa and Beyond
Source: Selamta (Ethiopian Airlines Magazine)
URL: https://selamta.ethiopianairlines.com/inspiration/a-whole-new-ball-game-womens-basketball-in-africa-and-beyond/

INTRODUCTION
The article highlights the rise of women’s basketball in Africa, its growing popularity, and the efforts to empower female athletes through professional leagues and grassroots programs.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT
Women’s basketball in Africa has evolved from limited participation to increasing visibility, driven by initiatives from FIBA Africa, BAL, and local federations. Historically, cultural and structural barriers restricted opportunities for women in sports.

KEY POINTS
- Growth of women’s basketball leagues and tournaments across Africa.
- Role of BAL and NBA Africa in promoting gender inclusion.
- Rising interest among young girls through grassroots programs.

SOCIAL IMPACT
Women’s basketball fosters empowerment, education, and gender equality. It challenges stereotypes and creates pathways for leadership and professional careers.

NOTABLE EXAMPLES
- Nigeria and Senegal leading in women’s basketball achievements.
- Players like Ezinne Kalu and Astou Traoré inspiring the next generation.
- Partnerships with NGOs and brands to support female athletes.

LOOKING AHEAD
The future of women’s basketball in Africa includes expanding leagues, increasing sponsorship, and integrating technology for global visibility.

CITATIONS
Source: Selamta Magazine, interviews with players and officials, FIBA Africa reports.
