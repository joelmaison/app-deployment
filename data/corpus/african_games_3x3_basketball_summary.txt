Title: 3x3 Basketball at the African Games
Source: Wikipedia
URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/3x3_basketball_at_the_African_Games

INTRODUCTION
3x3 basketball became an official discipline of the African Games starting in 2019, replacing traditional 5-on-5 basketball. The event occurs every four years, with editions held in Rabat (2019) and Accra (2023).

HISTORY
- First event: 2019 in Rabat, Morocco
- Latest event: 2023 in Accra, Ghana

MEN’S TOURNAMENT SUMMARIES
- 2019: Gold – Madagascar, Silver – Egypt, Bronze – Nigeria
- 2023: Gold – Algeria, Silver – Ghana, Bronze – Uganda

WOMEN’S TOURNAMENT SUMMARIES
- 2019: Gold – Nigeria, Silver – Mali, Bronze – DR Congo
- 2023: Gold – Mali, Silver – Nigeria, Bronze – DR Congo

MEDAL TABLE (COMBINED)
- Mali: 2 Gold, 1 Silver
- Nigeria: 1 Gold, 1 Silver, 1 Bronze
- Egypt: 1 Gold, 1 Silver
- Algeria: 1 Gold, 1 Bronze
- Madagascar: 1 Gold
- DR Congo: 2 Bronze
- Ghana: 1 Silver, 1 Bronze
- Others: Benin, Burkina Faso, Uganda with single medals in contests

PARTICIPATION DETAILS
- Men’s teams: 10 (2019), 16 (2023)
- Women’s teams: 12 (2019), 13 (2023)

SPECIAL CONTESTS (2023)
- Dunk Contest: Gold – Soumaila Sissouma (Mali)
- Shoot-out Contest: Gold – Habiba El-Gizawy (Egypt)

SIGNIFICANCE
The inclusion of 3x3 basketball reflects global trends and provides African nations with a fast-paced, urban-style competition platform.
