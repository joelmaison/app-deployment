Title: FIBA Africa – History and Development
Source: FIBA Official Website
URL: https://about.fiba.basketball/en/regions/africa/history

INTRODUCTION
FIBA Africa is the governing body for basketball in Africa, operating under FIBA since its establishment in 1961. It oversees continental competitions, development programs, and governance for national federations.

HISTORICAL CONTEXT
Founded in 1961, FIBA Africa began with a small number of member federations and has grown to include over 50 national associations. It organizes AfroBasket, club championships, and youth tournaments, playing a key role in basketball’s expansion across the continent.

KEY FACTS
- Year of establishment: 1961
- Headquarters: Abidjan, Côte d’Ivoire
- Member federations: 54
- Major competitions: AfroBasket, FIBA Africa Champions Cup, youth events

NOTABLE DEVELOPMENTS
FIBA Africa has introduced grassroots programs, coaching clinics, and referee training to strengthen basketball infrastructure. It also collaborates with BAL and NBA Africa to elevate professional opportunities for players.

MEDIA/TV PARTNERS
Coverage of AfroBasket and other events is provided by continental broadcasters and FIBA’s digital platforms, ensuring global visibility.

SOCIAL IMPACT
Through initiatives like Basketball for Good, FIBA Africa promotes education, gender equality, and youth empowerment across African communities.

NOTABLE TEAMS/PLAYERS
Teams: Angola, Nigeria, Senegal, Tunisia (dominant in AfroBasket history)
Players: Hakeem Olajuwon (Nigeria), Salah Mejri (Tunisia), Al-Farouq Aminu (Nigeria)

LOOKING AHEAD
FIBA Africa aims to expand youth development programs, enhance women’s basketball, and strengthen partnerships with BAL for professional growth.
